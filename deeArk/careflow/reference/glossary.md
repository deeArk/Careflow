# Glossary

Plain-language definitions of terms used across Careflow. Click an entry to expand it.

***

## A — F

<details>

<summary><strong>Admin</strong></summary>

The role with full system control. Admins manage users, monitor activity, and configure services and pricing.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Appointment</strong></summary>

A scheduled clinic visit created before the patient arrives. Appointments help staff plan the daily workload.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Cashier</strong></summary>

The role responsible for invoice generation, payment confirmation, and receipt issuance.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Consultation</strong></summary>

The clinical review stage where a doctor or clinician records the patient's complaints, diagnosis, and treatment information. Vitals may be recorded first on a separate intake screen.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Diagnosis</strong></summary>

The clinician's recorded medical conclusion for a patient visit.

</details>

***

## G — P

<details>

<summary><strong>Invoice</strong></summary>

An itemized billing record created for a patient visit. It can include consultation charges and assigned services.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Patient history</strong></summary>

The record of past visits, consultations, services, and payment activity linked to a patient.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Patient registration</strong></summary>

The process of creating or updating a patient profile before care begins.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Payment</strong></summary>

The recorded settlement of an invoice. A successful payment should update billing status and allow receipt generation.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Prescription</strong></summary>

Treatment instructions or medications recorded during consultation.

</details>

***

## Q — Z

<details>

<summary><strong>Queue</strong></summary>

The ordered list of patients waiting to be seen. Careflow supports real-time queue updates and priority handling.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Receipt</strong></summary>

Proof of payment issued after a cashier confirms a transaction.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Role-based access</strong></summary>

An access model where permissions depend on the user's assigned role, such as admin, receptionist, doctor, or cashier.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Service</strong></summary>

A medical or operational item assigned to a patient visit and usually included in billing.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Visit</strong></summary>

One outpatient encounter in the clinic, from registration through payment and completion.

</details>

<details>

<summary><strong>Vitals</strong></summary>

Basic intake measurements such as blood pressure, temperature, pulse, and weight. Staff usually record vitals before complaints and diagnosis are entered.

</details>
